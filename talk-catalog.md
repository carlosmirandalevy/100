# 100 Things You Can Do with AI (and Some You Shouldn't) — A Practical Session for Everyone

**Speaker:** Carlos Miranda Levy

---

## One-Paragraph Version

The conversation around AI too often centers on automation — doing the same things faster and easier. But that framing misses the point entirely. AI is not about replacing what we already do; it is about doing MORE and BETTER than before, and about learning to do new things and improve ourselves. In this hands-on session, Carlos Miranda Levy walks you through real, practical examples across writing, learning, creativity, music, video, coding, science, business, and family life — showing what becomes possible when you stop asking "what can AI do instead of me?" and start asking "what can I do *with* AI that I never could before?" You will leave with concrete ideas you can try today, a clear sense of what to avoid, and a new perspective on AI as a partner for growth — not a shortcut for the status quo.

---

## Three-Paragraph Version

The conversation around AI too often centers on automation — which jobs will disappear, which tasks machines will take over, how to do the same things faster and easier. But that framing misses the point entirely. AI is not about replacing what we already do. It is about doing MORE and BETTER than before. It is about learning to do new things and improve ourselves. When we shift from asking "what can AI do instead of me?" to "what can I do *with* AI that I never could before?", everything changes.

In this practical session, Carlos Miranda Levy draws from a curated collection of over 100 real examples — spanning writing, learning, art, music, video, productivity, coding, science, business, and family life — to show what becomes possible when human creativity meets AI as a partner. You will see how a beginner can compose music, how a parent can create personalized bedtime stories, how a professional can analyze data they never had the skills to tackle, and how a student can build things that once required entire teams. These are not hypothetical scenarios; they are things people are doing right now.

You will leave this session with concrete ideas you can try today, a clear understanding of what AI should *not* be used for, and — most importantly — a new perspective. AI is not a shortcut for the status quo. It is an invitation to grow, to reach further, and to discover capabilities you did not know you had.
